Jadyn Wilson

From a young age she gravitated towards anything creative until her first film class her sophomore year of high school changing her life. Loving the role of director making her final film for that class making a small film having a fun summer day with friends.

Right now she’s inspired by seeing black stories made by black filmmakers. amazed by the sinners movie seeing a dark part of our past turned into an amazing horror movie with an amazing plus size dark skin main love interest these types of films remind her to remain focused on her path and her journey.

Learning how to best tell stories with character development by properly setting her characters up for their emotional journey. Prioritizing the character’s evolution in the story over cool shots. Making sure you affect your story

Producing behind his eyes was a bit difficult for her because she has to do it through Columbia for her BFA thesis, so there were parts of the project that she couldnt control. Relying on those around her she realized that when producing the story you have to be at the forefront making big decisions on the story, and the portrayal of characters.

“Winning the Emmy help me realize that this is the path that I was meant to take, and being on set for behind his eyes solidified that this is what I wanna do for the rest of my life, making art with my my friends about underrepresented stories means the world to me, this entire entire experience was very rewarding. ￼”

Jadyn believes that hollywood’s biggest failure right now is casting, making sure that casting includes representation. Looking at Annie from the movie Sinners that is a perfect example for perfect casting for a love interest making her a strong character that effected the plot really well and being an inspiration to female viewers. She feels like there can be a better job done by hollywood when it comes to making sure that black and poc stories and perspectives are shown and that we dont have to take on the responsibility ourselves.

Jadyn feels that any themes or truths about the experience of being black should be told. Especially when you’re making media that shows that we as black people are more than the monolith stereotypes that still affect us to this day.

It was a bit difficult to film fighting and emotional scenes for her movie behind our eyes. Trying to direct and guide her actors to deal with and to work with deep scenes and navigate through the awkwardness.

But with her experience she wants to continue her journey with going into more genres, more music videos, action, maybe tv shows as well. But she is planning a tv show pilot that focuses on a plot that is extremely similar to her life growing up in a predominantly white area and going to school in a more urban area experiencing more cultures she didn't experience back home.
