Alex

Introduced to art by his grandfather who gave up his love for art so that he would be accepted by his family he was marrying into but would teach Alex how to draw.

Growing up in an unstable environment and lacking a proper male figure he got attached to super heroes. They taught him how to deal with his problems and was inspired to draw them.

How has your illustration style changed from when you first started to now?

“Well when I first got my first gallery downtown, my mom said " I can finally tell u this, before ur art was shit and I didn't want to discourage you but ur so much better now"

Fueled by their years of experience as an artist they’re on different level as a comic artist.

"My ex bf Danny was a recovering drug addict and he got to 4 months sober and got the chip and as a way to show him I was proud of him, I created a comic book of us and gave him to him, it was the first project I put a lot of work and so much of myself and vulnerability in I feel."

His next project will be a Scott Pilgrim style telling of their breakup and the journey.

When creating a comic he usually starts with the visuals and story idea. Figuring out what they’re going to do then make 10 rough draft pages of ideas. Next is designing then ink and finally color.

Their inspirations are Darick Robertson, Bob Kane, Stan Lee , Marcelo Ventura and Dave gibbons.

They would love in the future to work with a big company like marvel or DC crawling up in infamy as an artist. But the future does entail graphic novels, a video game, a cartoon or anything to get their name out there.
